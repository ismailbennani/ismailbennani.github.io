Yesterdays

  • 2019 - 2021
    QuickCheck testing of hybrid systems (dropped)

    I introduced a quantitative method to compare a program's execution with the properties expressed in the subset of Signal Temporal Logic (STL) that I defined during my internship. I utilized optimization techniques such as automatic differentiation and gradient descent to guide the random search for falsifying inputs. The outcome of this work is the zlscheck tool, which received the jury award in the Falsification category at the ARCH 2020 friendly competition [1].

    Tech: OCaml, Matlab

  • apr. to aug. 2018
    Test generation for synchronous and hybrid systems

    I developed an automated method for identifying inputs that can cause failures in safety-critical systems written in Zélus. I defined a subset of Signal Temporal Logic that is not only sufficient to represent most formulas used for specifying synchronous hybrid programs but also can be compiled into a synchronous program that verifies the truth of these formulas at each time step of the simulation.

    Tech: OCaml, Lustre, Sundials
